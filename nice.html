Nice 2026

Sunshine, Italian Facades & French Riviera Elegance

Top 10 Architectural & Scenic Sites in Nice

Promenade des Anglais

Iconic French Riviera promenade, perfect for a walk along the azure Mediterranean sea.

💡 Tip: Rent a "vélo bleu" to explore the full 7km stretch easily.

Vieux Nice

A labyrinth of narrow streets with vibrant Italian-style facades and hidden Baroque churches.

💡 Tip: Get lost in the alleys to find the best local olive oil shops.

Castle Hill

Offers the best panoramic view of the Bay of Angels and the rooftops of the old town.

💡 Tip: Use the free elevator if you want to skip the stairs to the top.

Matisse Museum

Located in a 17th-century Genoese villa, housing one of the world's largest Matisse collections.

💡 Tip: Walk through the surrounding Cimiez olive grove after your visit.

Cours Saleya

The social heart of the city, famous for its daily vibrant flower and food market.

💡 Tip: Visit on Mondays for the famous antiques and flea market.

Russian Cathedral

The largest Eastern Orthodox cathedral in Western Europe, featuring spectacular architecture.

💡 Tip: Remember to dress modestly to enter this sacred site.

Marc Chagall Museum

A national museum dedicated to the biblical works of the modernist master Marc Chagall.

💡 Tip: The garden and the stained-glass concert hall are must-sees.

MAMAC

The Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art, featuring avant-garde works from the 1950s to today.

💡 Tip: Go to the rooftop for a unique 360-degree view of Nice.

Place Masséna

The main square of Nice, known for its red buildings, checkerboard floor, and modern statues.

💡 Tip: Beautiful at night when the "Jaume Plensa" statues light up.

Port Lympia

The historic port of Nice, filled with traditional colorful wooden boats known as "pointus."

💡 Tip: Perfect spot for a sunset drink while watching the ferries.

Top 10 Culinary Experiences in Nice

Le Chantecler

Located in the Negresco hotel, it offers the peak of 2-star Michelin luxury in a grand setting.

💰 Price: Luxury. The ultimate fine dining in Nice.

La Petite Maison

Famous for high-end Provençal cuisine and its celebrity-filled, lively atmosphere.

💰 Price: Luxury. Best for upscale Niçois specialties.

Chez Pipo

The most authentic place to try "Socca," the famous chickpea pancake of Nice.

💰 Price: Cheap. A historic budget-friendly must-visit.

Jan

A unique Michelin-star experience where South African flavors meet Riviera ingredients.

💰 Price: Luxury / Exclusive. Modern and creative.

Fenocchio

A mythical glacier in the old town famous for flavors like lavender, thyme, and tomato.

💰 Price: Budget-friendly. Iconic ice cream experience.

Le Bistrot d'Antoine

Market-fresh traditional food in a cozy bistro setting. Very popular with locals.

💰 Price: Mid-range. Outstanding value for money.

Achiardi

A family-run gem serving authentic local specialties away from the tourist crowds.

💰 Price: Moderate. Pure Niçois soul on a plate.

Olive & Artichaut

Features a "Cuisine Niçoise" label, serving seasonal food directly from the market.

💰 Price: Mid-range. Fresh and artisanal.

Le Plongeoir

Dine on a pillar over the sea rocks. One of the most scenic restaurant locations in France.

💰 Price: High-end. Unbeatable Mediterranean views.

Confiserie Florian

Famous for their crystallized flowers and citrus jams. A sweet piece of history.

💰 Price: Moderate. Perfect for gourmet gifts.

Ultimate Nice Travel Itineraries – 1, 3 & 7 Day Guides

⏱️ 1-Day Express

Best of Nice in a Day

  • 09:00: Start at Cours Saleya Market – breakfast among flowers.
  • 11:00: Explore Vieux Nice (Old Town) and its hidden churches.
  • 13:00: Lunch at Chez Pipo for authentic Socca.
  • 14:30: Walk up Castle Hill for the best views of the city.
  • 16:30: Stroll the Promenade des Anglais towards Hotel Negresco.
  • 19:00: Dinner at Le Bistrot d'Antoine in the Old Town.
🥂 Weekend Getaway

3-Day Riviera Highlights

  • Day 1: Old Town, Cours Saleya Market, and sunset at Castle Hill.
  • Day 2: Art morning at Matisse & Chagall Museums. Afternoon at Place Masséna.
  • Day 3: Day trip to nearby Villefranche-sur-Mer or the village of Eze.
🏰 7-Day Immersion

The Full French Riviera Experience

  • Day 1: Nice highlights – Promenade, Old Town, Port Lympia.
  • Day 2: Nice Art Museums (Matisse, Chagall, MAMAC).
  • Day 3: Coastal train to Monaco and Monte Carlo.
  • Day 4: Boat trip or bus to Saint-Paul-de-Vence (art village).
  • Day 5: Day trip to Cannes and Antibes.
  • Day 6: Relaxing day at a beach club in Nice or Saint-Jean-Cap-Ferrat.
  • Day 7: Local cooking class in Nice and farewell dinner at Le Plongeoir.
